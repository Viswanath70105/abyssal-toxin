# Gideon Stroud (Station Director)

## The Crime
Initiated Protocol Omega (the scuttling sequence) to sink the station and bury the crew alive.

## The Shame
He is not a villain; he is a martyr. When Corporate ordered him to secure the organism at all costs, Gideon saw the unredacted files on what the mutagen does. He realized that if even a single spore reached the surface, it would be an extinction-level event for Earth. 
He didn't initiate the scuttling sequence for a payout or a promotion. He did it to ensure the organism never left the ocean floor. He condemned forty innocent people—including himself—to die in the dark so that his family, and 8 billion others on the surface, could live. He carries the unbearable guilt of a mass murderer who did the right thing.

## The Tragedy (Player Discovery)
When the player finally breaks into his private terminal, they expect to find offshore bank accounts or greedy corporate emails. Instead, they find a half-written, unsent letter to his children, apologizing for not coming home, alongside a simulation he ran 100 times trying to find a way to save the crew. Every simulation ended in global contamination. He had no choice.
