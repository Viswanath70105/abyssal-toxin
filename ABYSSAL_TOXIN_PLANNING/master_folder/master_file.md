## Core Premise
*Abyssal Toxin* is a 15-chapter hybrid of a deductive thriller and survival horror. The player's ultimate reward is not just catching the culprit, but finally understanding the entire horrific chain of events. The central question is: **"Who deliberately caused the catastrophe?"**
The truth is buried beneath layers of suspicion. The player must navigate failing station sectors, a manipulative biological threat, and a crew where everyone is hiding both a Crime and a Shame.

## The Threat: The Abyssal Organism
The organism is not just a monster; it is a character. It learns, imitates, and remembers conversations. It actively manipulates the investigation. By the later chapters, it will begin using the voices of dead crew members (like Miller) over the comms—sometimes luring you to your death, sometimes leading you to actual evidence.

## Characters (The Suspects), Secrets, & Indirect Evidence
Every suspect has **Two Secrets**: a **Crime** (sabotage, theft, deletion of data) and a **Shame** (a personal failing). Everyone is important to the timeline, and everyone carries a piece of the tragedy. 
*Note: Clues are Indirect Evidence that must be found and linked in the Detective Notebook.*

### 1. Gideon Stroud - Station Director / Corporate Overseer
* **Role:** The final human obstacle. **He never lies.** When confronted, he simply admits to his actions. The terrifying mystery becomes *why* he did it, not *if* he did it.
* **The Crime:** Initiated Protocol Omega (the scuttling sequence) to sink the station and bury the crew alive.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** He is not a villain; he is a martyr. When Corporate ordered him to secure the organism at all costs, Gideon saw the unredacted files on what the mutagen does. He realized that if even a single spore reached the surface, it would be an extinction-level event for Earth. He condemned forty innocent people—including himself—to die in the dark so that his family and 8 billion others on the surface could live. He carries the unbearable guilt of a mass murderer who did the right thing.
* **Arc:** Starts Stoic and Authoritative -> Becomes Truthful and Unapologetic -> Ends as a Resolute Martyr.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Unsent Transmission to Earth Defense (Personal Terminal):** "The organism is highly adaptive and hostile... I am executing the final failsafe."
    * **Scuttling Protocol Auth Log (Command Center):** Shows the scuttling sequence was triggered via Gideon's personal biometrics.
    * **A Faded Family Photo (Gideon's Quarters):** Note on back: "I'm sorry. I have to keep you safe from this. Don't look for me."

### 2. Dr. Aris - Lead Medical Officer
* **Role:** The player's main ally for mutagen analysis, but deeply anxious and hiding her own panic.
* **The Crime:** Forged medical reports and covered up the initial signs of the mutagen outbreak among the crew.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** She requested specialized medicine, but Corporate denied it. Desperate, she forged the crew's health reports so Corporate wouldn't cut their hazard pay. She believed she could synthesize a cure herself in secret before anyone noticed. Her hubris and desperate delay doomed them all.
* **Arc:** Starts Confident and Professional -> Becomes Obsessive and Defensive -> Accepts failure -> Finally tells the truth and cooperates.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Mislabeled Vials (Medbay Cold Storage):** Blood samples labeled "Severe Vitamin D Deficiency" that are violently mutating.
    * **Rejected Medical Requisition (Medbay Terminal):** "Denied. Too expensive. Treat with local supplies and keep the drill running."
    * **Personal Audio Log (Dr. Aris's PDA):** "It's adapting faster than I can synthesize a counter-agent. If I report this now, they'll just seal the sector with us inside. I can fix this. I just need more time."

### 3. Miller - Chief Mechanic
* **Role:** The emotional anchor and mentor. His death in Chapter 2 is a deep emotional wound. The organism imitates him later.
* **The Crime:** Ignored critical structural warnings and remained silent about the station's failing infrastructure.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** Miller knew the lower bulkheads were failing. Protocol dictated an immediate evacuation, meaning the crew would lose their hazard pay. Miller's daughter on the surface has a rare, expensive illness. He silenced alarms and patched the bulkheads to get his final paycheck. His desperate gamble as a father allowed the drill to breach the cavern.
* **Arc:** Starts as a Friendly Mentor -> Becomes Stressed and Deflecting -> Dies carrying Guilt -> Becomes a horrifying voice for the Organism.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Bypassed Relay Switch (Lower Maintenance shaft):** A physical piece of the station's alarm system, deliberately fried. Clean sabotage.
    * **Overdue Medical Bills (Miller's Locker):** Invoices from an Earth-side hospital for a rare pediatric treatment. A final notice of care termination.
    * **Denied Leave Request (HR Terminal):** "We need you here, Miller. Complete the cycle, get the hazard bonus. It’s the only way."

### 4. Arthur - The Engineer
* **Role:** The grumpy, veteran technician who knows every system but keeps disappearing during emergencies.
* **The Crime:** Sabotaged the deep-sea drill and bypassed safety regulators, directly causing the cavern breach.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** Ten years ago, his strict adherence to corporate protocol caused a younger worker to get trapped and die. On Nereus Station, he secretly sabotaged the drill's telemetry to make it look like they hit bedrock, hoping Corporate would cancel the dig and send everyone home safely. Instead, the sabotage caused the drill to violently misalign and plunge into the abyssal cavern.
* **Arc:** Starts Defensive and Avoidant -> Becomes Angry -> Accepts guilt and admits sabotage -> Dies repairing the reactor (if forgiven).
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Drill Telemetry Data (Engineering Terminal):** Shows a manual override of the drill's torque limiters.
    * **Therapy Transcripts (Medbay Records):** Details severe PTSD. "I can hear the rocks groaning again. They're pushing us too fast. Someone's going to die again."
    * **Half-Burned Manifesto (Incinerator Room):** "Corporate won't listen. The core is unstable. I have to break the drill. A week of downtime is better than all of us dead."

### 5. David - The Security Officer
* **Role:** Controls the cameras and locked doors, acting as the station's enforcer.
* **The Crime:** Deleted surveillance footage of Sector 4 and the Mess Hall over the last 48 hours.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** Terminally ill. To afford a comfortable life for his family after he's gone, he had been disabling cameras to siphon rare earth minerals to sell on the black market. By hiding his petty theft, he unknowingly destroyed the only early warning signs of the outbreak.
* **Arc:** Starts Cold and Authoritative -> Becomes Paranoid and Secretive -> Breaks down -> Becomes Protective and sacrifices himself.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Server Wipe Audit (Security Hub):** Shows David's credentials were used to scrub all camera feeds in the lower decks at the exact time the infection breached.
    * **Hidden Stash of Abyssal Quartz (Armory Vents):** Packaged for smuggling.
    * **Medical Diagnosis (David's Quarters):** Stage 4 terminal illness. "Six months left. Get your affairs in order."

### 6. Chloe - The Comms Technician
* **Role:** Intercepts messages and manages the station's radio tower. Highly paranoid.
* **The Crime:** Intercepted and deleted a horrific distress call from the initial drill team before they went silent.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** Protocol required her to log the call and join the rescue team. Paralyzed by pure cowardice, she deleted the log and claimed it was static. The drill team died waiting for a rescue she ensured would never come. She listens to their screams every night to punish herself.
* **Arc:** Starts Paranoid and Jumpy -> Denies everything -> Crushed by shame -> Finds courage to broadcast warning.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **The 'Ghost' Audio File (Chloe's Personal Data Drive):** The original, horrific distress call from the drill team being slaughtered.
    * **Comms Relay Log (Communications Tower):** Shows an incoming emergency channel was manually muted.
    * **Empty Prescription Bottles (Chloe's Quarters):** Numerous bottles of heavy sedatives and sleeping pills. She hasn't slept naturally since the breach.

### 7. Victor - The Xenobiologist
* **Role:** The brilliant scientist locked in quarantine. Intellectually honest, fascinated by the organism.
* **The Crime:** Smuggled living samples of the abyssal mutagen into unauthorized ventilation shafts for testing.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** Diagnosed with early-onset dementia. They discovered the mutagen regenerates dead cellular tissue. They were desperately testing the mutagen on station rats, hoping to synthesize a cure for their own failing brain.
* **Arc:** Starts Eccentric and Fascinated -> Becomes Confused and Vulnerable -> Desperately confesses -> Accepts his doom peacefully.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Altered Ventilation Schematics (Biology Lab):** Vents leading from the lab to the lower decks are highlighted, mapping out where he released infected subjects.
    * **Neurological Scans (Victor's Desk):** Showing rapid deterioration. Margin notes: "Losing time. Forgetting names. Need a solution."
    * **Research Journal on Cellular Regeneration:** "The organism doesn't just infect; it repairs and reanimates dead tissue. If synthesized correctly, it could repair my neural pathways."

### 8. Otto Finch - The Cook
* **Role:** Harmless appearance, controls the mess hall, but constantly claims they are out of supplies.
* **The Crime:** Systematically stealing and hoarding food rations and medkits, causing station-wide shortages.
* **The Shame (Backstory):** He discovered a stowaway hiding in a cargo pod (Maya). Otto has been starving himself to keep her alive. Because she explored the lower decks searching for her brother, she unknowingly tracked dormant spores into the ventilation system.
* **Arc:** Starts Genial and Harmless -> Becomes Nervous and Protective -> Pleads for Maya's safety -> Becomes fiercely loyal.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Inventory Discrepancies (Mess Hall Terminal):** Large quantities of high-calorie rations missing.
    * **A Child's Drawing (Pantry Shelves):** Depicts a monster and a "nice cook" giving out food.
    * **Makeshift Bed (Dry Storage Room):** A nest made of blankets, way too small for Otto.

### 9. Maya - The Stowaway
* **The Crime:** Tracked dormant spores into the ventilation system.
* **The Shame:** She was searching the lower decks looking for her dead brother.
* **Arc:** Starts Hidden and Fearful -> Terrified of discovery -> Innocent sorrow -> Trusting the player.
* **Clues to Find:**
    * **Small Muddy Footprints (Ventilation Shafts):** Tracks leading from the deep-sea airlocks into the station's core.
    * **Circled Crew Manifest (Maya's Hideout):** A name from the drill team is heavily circled, with the word "WHERE?"
    * **Discarded Child's Jacket (Near the Breach Site):** The hem of the jacket is glowing faintly with the bioluminescence of the mutagen spores.

## Gamification Systems

### 1. The Detective Notebook & Indirect Evidence
* **Indirect Evidence (No Free Confessions):** Real people don't confess trauma just because you ask nicely. The player must assemble the Crimes and Shames from indirect evidence (listed above). A confession only confirms what the player has already deduced.
* The player maintains a notebook UI to collect these Clues (Timeline, Relationships, Contradictions).
* The player must manually link pieces of Indirect Evidence to a Suspect's Statement to break their alibi.
* **The Missing Page:** There is one blank page at the end of the notebook. Once enough deductions are linked, the player finally writes "The Truth" on this page.

### 2. The Accusation Mechanic
The "Accuse" dialogue option only appears when the player has slotted the correct combination of Indirect Evidence into their Detective Notebook to form a logical hypothesis. However, the player can make premature or incorrect accusations if they misinterpret the evidence.

* **Penalty for False Accusation:**
    * **Trust Broken:** The character becomes hostile and refuses to provide access codes.
    * **Time Wasted:** The station decays further. A sector may flood, destroying undiscovered clues.
    * **Organism Exploitation:** The organism feeds on negative emotion, targeting the falsely accused for hallucinations or assimilation.

* **Minor Accusations (Early Game):** E.g., Accusing Otto Finch. Correct Deduction keeps his secret and gains loyalty. Incorrect Deduction locks him up, leaving Maya to starve or be infected.
* **Mid-Game Accusations (Chapters 6-10):** E.g., Accusing Arthur of the cavern breach. Correct Deduction (understanding his PTSD) allows you to forgive him, and he sacrifices himself later to vent the reactor. Incorrect Deduction causes a mental breakdown, turning him into a host for the organism.
* **The Final Accusation (Chapter 15):** The climax. Confronting Gideon Stroud about the scuttling protocol. You realize he is the only one who made a calculated decision to kill everyone to save Earth. You must choose to agree with him (Martyr's Path), fight him (Survivor's Path), or, if heavily infected, kill him to spread the organism (Infected Path).

### 3. Red Herrings (Real Clues, Wrong Interpretations)
To maintain the mystery, the game uses real clues that naturally lead the player to incorrect conclusions if they stop digging too early.
*   *Example Clue:* **Deleted Security Footage.**
*   *Wrong Interpretation:* David murdered Miller or sabotaged the station to cover up the biological outbreak. (If you accuse him of this, you fail the deduction).
*   *Actual Truth:* David deleted the footage weeks earlier because he was smuggling minerals. Same clue, different meaning.

### 4. Infection (Hidden Ticking Clock)
The infection percentage is **never shown to the player**. It is hidden and communicated entirely through symptoms and gameplay changes:
* **Mild:** Occasional coughing in text descriptions.
* **Moderate:** Vision briefly blurs; dialogue portraits distort for a split second. NPCs point out you are shaking.
* **Severe:** Dialogue options literally disappear because you forget the words. Trust decreases automatically.
* **Critical:** The organism starts speaking directly to your mind. Complete hallucination.

### 5. Station Decay (Time Pressure)
The station physically deteriorates, permanently restricting access to evidence.
* **Chapter 3:** Sector B accessible.
* **Chapter 6:** Sector B flooded forever (clues inside are lost).
* **Chapter 8:** Power gone (requires flashlight/flare).
* **Chapter 11:** Reactor unstable.

### 6. The Fairness Audit
*Every major revelation in the game must pass the Fairness Audit.*
*   **Question:** Could an intelligent player solve this BEFORE the reveal?
*   If "No" -> The design cheated. The twist is unearned.
*   If "Yes, but it's difficult" -> Perfect. Every major twist (e.g., Gideon scuttling the station, Maya tracking the spores, Arthur breaking the drill) must feel inevitable in hindsight because the clues were always there.

## Dialogue & Tone Examples

### Scene 1: Interrogating Arthur (The Engineer)
**Context:** Player corners Arthur in the Engineering Bay after finding Drill Telemetry and Therapy Transcripts.
**[PLAYER]:** "The logs don't lie, Arthur. You manually overrode the torque limiters. You shattered the cavern wall."
**[ARTHUR]:** *(Deflecting, sweating profusely)* "I—I don't know what you're looking at. The telemetry is corrupted."
**[PLAYER]:** "The timestamp is from *before* the breach. You did it on purpose."
**[ARTHUR]:** *(Anger flaring)* "You think I wanted this? Corporate was pushing us too hard! I told Gideon the rocks were groaning!"
**[PLAYER]:** "It wasn't just about this dig, was it? You were terrified. The collapse on Titan-4... you thought it was happening again."
**[ARTHUR]:** *(Freezes, hollow exhaustion)* "...I just wanted to buy us a week of downtime. If the drill was broken, they'd have to let us rest. I didn't know the cavern wall was that thin."

### Scene 2: The Organism's Taunt (Miller's Voice)
**Context:** Player is highly infected (UI glitching). Walking through a flooded corridor.
**[UNKNOWN_SPEAKER]:** "...kid? You there? It's Miller."
**[PLAYER]:** "Miller? No... no, I saw you die in the airlock."
**[MILLER_VOICE]:** "Yeah. It hurt, kid. It hurt a lot. Why didn't you cycle the door?" *(Text begins to visually distort)*
**[PLAYER]:** "You're not him. You're the infection."
**[MILLER_VOICE]:** *(Voice shifts to a wet, echoing distortion)* "I aM eVeryOne nOw. I caN tAsTe hiS mEmoireS... sHe is gOing tO dIe alOne beCause yOu wEre tOo sLow."

### Scene 3: Confronting Dr. Aris (Lead Medical)
**Context:** Player discovers the Mislabeled Vials.
**[PLAYER]:** "Vitamin D deficiency, Aris? Really? These blood samples are practically eating through the glass."
**[DR. ARIS]:** "Of course I lied! Do you know what Corporate would have done if I told them we had a Class-9 biological contaminant? They would have sent an incineration squad!"
**[PLAYER]:** "You should have warned us."
**[DR. ARIS]:** "I am the only person on this rig with a doctorate in deep-sea pathology. I was so close. The mutagen is a puzzle, and I was one sequence away from breaking it!"
**[PLAYER]:** *(Present Rejected Medical Requisition)* "You didn't have the supplies. Corporate denied your request. You were out of time, and you refused to admit it."
**[DR. ARIS]:** *(Ego shatters)* "...I couldn't just let them burn us. I thought I was smart enough to beat it with what we had."

## Narrative Structure (15 Chapters & Revelations)

*   **Chapter 1:** Arrival. Meet everyone. Miller acts as your mentor.
    *   *Revelation:* The station is already breaking down; Corporate doesn't care.
*   **Chapter 2:** Death of Miller. Lockdown initiated.
    *   *Revelation:* The station was locked down intentionally, not by an automated system.
*   **Chapter 3:** Learn station layout and begin early sleuthing.
    *   *Revelation:* Someone erased the security logs from the last 24 hours.
*   **Chapter 4:** First Interrogations. (Minor Accusation).
    *   *Revelation:* Everyone's alibi overlaps in a way that proves someone is lying.
*   **Chapter 5:** A second body drops.
    *   *Revelation:* The victim didn't eat the food. It wasn't food poisoning. The entire working theory is wrong.
*   **Chapter 6:** Quarantine Access. Meet the Xenobiologist. Sector B floods.
    *   *Revelation:* Corporate was drilling for this organism intentionally.
*   **Chapter 7:** Interrogations deepen. Player links lies in the notebook.
    *   *Revelation:* A trusted ally (e.g., Dr. Aris) has been forging reports.
*   **Chapter 8:** Power failure. The organism mimics Miller's voice. (Mid-Game Accusation).
    *   *Revelation:* The organism remembers conversations and is manipulating the investigation.
*   **Chapter 9:** Corporate secrets uncovered (Omega Protocol).
    *   *Revelation:* The station was doomed by Corporate before Miller even died.
*   **Chapter 10:** The pieces come together. Navigating the decaying, pitch-black station.
    *   *Revelation:* The true sequence of events clicks into place on the Missing Page.
*   **Chapter 11:** The Reveal. Gideon Stroud answers truthfully.
    *   *Revelation:* Gideon isn't lying. He is trying to save the surface world.
*   **Chapter 12:** Everything collapses. Structural failure.
    *   *Revelation:* The organism is actively tearing the station apart to stop the scuttling.
*   **Chapter 13:** The escape sequence begins. Half the station is destroyed.
    *   *Revelation:* The player must choose who to save and who to leave behind.
*   **Chapter 14:** Final confrontation at the Bathysphere with Gideon Stroud.
    *   *Revelation:* The ultimate moral choice—destroy the evidence, or let it surface?
*   **Chapter 15:** The Endings.

## The 9 Endings

**1. The Whistleblower (Best Ending)**
* **Description:** Escape, expose the Corporation's conspiracy, survivors rescued.
* **Conditions:** Identify Gideon Stroud, save multiple crewmates, gather all major evidence, escape via Bathysphere.

**2. The Corporate Asset**
* **Description:** Take the deal, escape with Stroud, get rich. The world never knows.
* **Conditions:** Confront Stroud but agree to his payoff, wipe mainframe logs, escape via Bathysphere.

**3. Abyssal Madness (Transformed)**
* **Description:** Succumb to the mutagen and embrace the deep, surviving in a transformed state.
* **Conditions:** High hidden Infection level, "befriend" the organism, fail to board the pod.

**4. Crush Depth (Worst Ending)**
* **Description:** Failure to escape. The station implodes, everyone dies in the dark.
* **Conditions:** Fail critical skill checks during Chapter 13 or run out of time.

**5. The Sacrifice**
* **Description:** Stay behind to manually detonate the reactor, destroying the organism while crewmates escape.
* **Conditions:** Gather technical specs, high Trust with Engineer, choose to stay behind.

**6. The Quarantine**
* **Description:** Escape but captured by Corporate recovery teams. Imprisoned as a biohazard.
* **Conditions:** Escape via Bathysphere, high hidden Infection level, insufficient evidence to expose Corporate.

**7. New Abyss**
* **Description:** Broadcast the organism’s existence on an open channel before dying.
* **Conditions:** Fatal injuries, successfully route power to Comms Relay and broadcast, high Trust with Comms Technician.

**8. Mutual Destruction**
* **Description:** Confront Stroud, neither reaches the pod. Both fall into water. Bathysphere launches empty with black box data.
* **Conditions:** Physical struggle at Bathysphere, refuse deal but fail combat checks, black box data placed in pod beforehand.

**9. The Wrong Truth (The False Detective)**
* **Description:** You accuse the wrong person (e.g., the Engineer) with plausible evidence. The game accepts it. Corporate agrees. Months later, a news report reveals another station has gone silent. You failed.
* **Conditions:** Reach the Final Accusation but formally accuse an innocent NPC using circumstantial evidence on the Missing Page.
