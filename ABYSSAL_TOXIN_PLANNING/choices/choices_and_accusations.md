# Choices & Accusations Design

This document outlines the branching consequences and the core mechanics of the Accusation System in *Abyssal Toxin*.

---

## 1. The Accusation Mechanic
In *Abyssal Toxin*, the player cannot randomly guess who is guilty. The "Accuse" dialogue option only appears when the player has slotted the correct combination of **Indirect Evidence** into their Detective Notebook to form a logical hypothesis. 

However, the player *can* make premature or incorrect accusations if they misinterpret the evidence (e.g., accusing someone of the primary sabotage when they only committed a petty crime).

### The Penalty for False Accusation
If the player accuses a character without fully understanding their **Shame**, or if they accuse them of the wrong **Crime**:
1.  **Trust Broken:** The character becomes hostile and will refuse to provide further testimony or access codes for the rest of the game.
2.  **Time Wasted:** The station's structural integrity decays further. A sector may flood, permanently destroying any undiscovered clues inside.
3.  **Organism Exploitation:** The organism feeds on the negative emotion. If you falsely accuse someone, their stress spikes. The organism is more likely to target them for hallucinations, or even assimilate them if they isolate themselves in anger.

## 2. Minor Accusations (Chapters 1-5)
These are early-game confrontations that resolve sub-plots but don't solve the core mystery. They teach the player the consequences of their choices.

### A. Otto Finch (The Food Hoarding)
*   **The Choice:** Confront Otto about the missing rations.
*   **Correct Deduction (With Evidence of Maya):** You realize he is hiding a child. You can choose to keep his secret or report it to Security. Keeping the secret gains Otto's fierce loyalty; he provides you with extra healing items later.
*   **Incorrect Deduction (Thinking he is just greedy):** You accuse him of stealing for himself. He gets locked in the brig. Maya starves or is forced into the vents looking for food, making her highly susceptible to the organism.

### B. David (The Deleted Footage)
*   **The Choice:** Confront David about the server wipe.
*   **Correct Deduction (With Evidence of his illness/smuggling):** You realize he was just hiding his smuggling to support his family, not aiding the organism. You can help him secure the payload in exchange for overriding security doors later.
*   **Incorrect Deduction (Thinking he is infected/saboteur):** You accuse him of working for the organism. He becomes paranoid, locks himself in the Armory, and refuses to open the weapons cache when the organism attacks in Chapter 8.

## 3. Mid-Game Accusations (Chapters 6-10)
These accusations deal with the catastrophic events that doomed the station, but still aren't the *ultimate* truth.

### A. Arthur (The Cavern Breach)
*   **The Choice:** Confront Arthur about sabotaging the drill.
*   **Correct Deduction (Understanding his PTSD):** You realize he broke the drill to stop the dig, accidentally causing the breach. 
    *   *Choice - Arrest:* You lock him up. He survives, but you lose his engineering expertise to fix the failing oxygen scrubbers in Chapter 11.
    *   *Choice - Forgive:* He works furiously to make amends, sacrificing himself to manually vent the reactor and save the remaining crew.
*   **Incorrect Deduction (Thinking he wanted to kill everyone):** He has a complete mental breakdown, flees into the flooded sectors, and becomes a horrifying host for the organism.

### B. Dr. Aris (The Medical Cover-up)
*   **The Choice:** Confront Aris about hiding the initial infection.
*   **Correct Deduction (Understanding her hubris):** You break her ego. She realizes she can't cure it alone. She hands over her unfinished research, which acts as a massive buff against the player's Infection UI later.
*   **Incorrect Deduction (Accusing her of creating the organism):** She becomes defensive and erratic. She accidentally injects herself with a flawed, mutated vaccine in a desperate attempt to prove you wrong, becoming a boss encounter.

---

## 4. The Final Accusation (Chapter 15)
The climax of the game. The station is collapsing. The organism is everywhere. Only Gideon Stroud remains between the player and the escape pods.

### Gideon Stroud (The Scuttling Protocol)
By the end, the player has realized that none of the other crew members *intentionally* caused the apocalyptic scenario. They made mistakes based on their flaws (Shame). Gideon is the only one who made a cold, calculated decision to kill everyone.

*   **The Final Choice:**
    *   **Option A: The Martyr's Path (Agree with Gideon).** You review all the evidence of the organism's adaptability. You realize Gideon is right. If a single spore reaches Earth, humanity is dead. You stand down, accept your fate, and watch the ocean crush the station. *Earns the "Abyssal Grave" Ending.*
    *   **Option B: The Survivor's Path (Fight Gideon).** You refuse to die for corporate mistakes. You must use everything you've learned—every code, every bypassed system, every ally you haven't alienated—to bypass his lockdown, defeat him, and launch the only working escape pod. *Earns the "Surface Tension" Ending.*
    *   **Option C: The Infected Path (If Infection Level > 80%).** Your dialogue options are entirely replaced by the organism's thoughts. You don't argue with Gideon; you just kill him. You take the escape pod, not to survive, but to bring the infection to the surface. *Earns the "Patient Zero" Ending.*
